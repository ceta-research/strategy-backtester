## TL;DR

Strategy is mechanically working as designed. **Three structural concerns:**

1. **Live drawdown is unrecovered.** -24% from Jan-2025 peak (₹119.26M → ₹90.63M). 441 days deep, no recovery yet. The 2025 yearly -16.3% is happening live.
2. **Extreme PnL concentration on a few names + a few years.** Top 10 instruments = 50.5% of profit; top 20 = 81.5%. Adani group alone ≈ 20.5% of profit. 5 years (2017, 2020, 2021, 2023, 2024) account for ALL lifetime profit; 12 other years net zero.
3. **422 of 758 instruments (55.7%) had negative PnL contribution.** Total drag from losers: -₹129M (vs +₹93M net). Strategy is "buy lottery tickets" — most lose, the few winners pay for everything.

These are characteristics of momentum strategies, not bugs. But they mean forward-return depends entirely on the next batch of mega-winners showing up.

### ⚠️ Slippage NOT modeled

Per `docs/archive/pre-engine-2026-03/REPO_AUDIT.md`: **"Engine pipeline has ZERO slippage. All 21 signal generators."**

Real-world impact estimate:
- Avg position value ≈ ₹833K (₹10M / 12 avg positions)
- 5 bps round-trip slippage ≈ ₹833 × 2 = ₹1,666 per trade
- 1642 trades × ₹1,666 = **~₹2.7M unaccounted cost**
- Reduces total profit from ₹93M → ~₹90M (a 3% reduction, ~30bps annualized)
- True CAGR likely ~14.9% rather than 15.2%

## 13. Optimization angles that haven't been tested

These are concrete, not handwave:

### A. Quality + sector overlay (highest impact)
- Champion has NO quality filter (no ROE/PE), NO sector cap.
- `enhanced_breakout` (cousin strategy with quality+sector) has Cal 0.656 vs eod_breakout's 0.446.
- **Hypothesis:** add `consecutive_positive_years >= 2` + `sector_max=4` to eod_breakout. Expected: -2-3pp CAGR, +30-50% Cal lift, lower regime fragility.
- **Effort:** 30-config sweep, single session.

### B. Regime-conditional TSL
- Current: 8% TSL always. In 2025, would 5% TSL have prevented the -24% drawdown?
- **Hypothesis:** when `direction_score < 0.45` (bear regime), tighten TSL to 5%. Otherwise keep 8%.
- **Effort:** signal-gen change + ~20-config A/B.

### C. ndh × ndma extended grid
- R2 only swept ndh ∈ [2,3,5,7] and ndm ∈ [3,5,10]. Both at low end.
- Untested: ndh=10, 15, 20, 30 (longer breakout windows); ndm=20, 50.
- **Hypothesis:** longer windows = fewer false signals = better hit rate, lower turnover.
- **Effort:** 50-config sweep, single session.

### D. Position sizing alternatives
- Current: equal weight (₹10M / 15 = ₹667K each).
- Untested: vol-targeted (smaller positions in higher-vol stocks), Kelly-fractional (current Kelly = 0.166 → 16.6% per position vs 6.7% equal-weight).
- **Hypothesis:** vol-targeting reduces tail risk; Kelly-fractional increases compounding.
- **Effort:** signal-gen change + sweep.

### E. Anomalous-drop threshold tightening
- Current: 20% gap triggers force-exit at 80% of last close.
- Top 5 losers had -22% to -31% returns. Tightening threshold to 15% might catch some.
- **Hypothesis:** marginal — most blow-ups happen on ndh entry day, not gap-down.
- **Effort:** trivial config change + re-run.

## 14. Recommended next-step

The 2025 live drawdown is the elephant. **Before any more parameter tuning, answer:**

1. Is the regime really turning (bear/sideways for next 3-5 years)? If yes, current champion is over-fit to 2019-2024.
2. If you optimize parameters now, the optimization will fit to "2010-2025 with -24% live drawdown" — fine for backtest, but the 2025 turn IS the OOS test.
3. **The honest experiment:** treat 2025-onward as TRUE OOS (held out from all sweeps). Re-pick champion using ONLY 2010-2024 data. Then test whether the new champion's 2025 forward performance is better than current champion's actual 2025 (-16.3%).

If new-champion-2025 ≥ -10%: optimization angle A/B/C/D worth pursuing.
If new-champion-2025 ≤ -15%: strategy is regime-fragile across params, not just at champion. Need ensemble or different mechanism.

This is the most informative single experiment you can run on `eod_breakout` right now.
