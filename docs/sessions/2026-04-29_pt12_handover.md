## What was tested

### Internal regime on eod_b (replaces NIFTYBEES)

| Approach | CAGR | MDD | Sharpe | Calmar |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| NIFTYBEES SMA100 + force-exit (prior best) | 19.73% | -27.52% | 1.320 | 0.717 |
| **IR SMA50/0.4 + force-exit, mh=7** | **20.55%** | -22.91% | 1.342 | 0.897 |
| **IR SMA50/0.4 + force-exit, no mh** | 20.21% | **-18.91%** | **1.366** | **1.069** |
| IR entry-only (no force-exit) | 17.62% | -24.96% | 1.202 | 0.706 |
| IR entry-only + adaptive TSL 30/3 | 14.93% | -25.38% | 0.991 | 0.588 |
| Hybrid (IR entry + NIFTYBEES exit) | 16.72% | -22.41% | 1.211 | 0.746 |

Key findings:
- Force-exit from internal regime works because it's self-referencing —
  when the strategy's own universe turns bearish, that IS the signal.
- Adaptive TSL is NOT a substitute for force-exit (different purpose).
- Removing min_hold unlocks faster regime exits → MDD drops 4pp.
- 1yr gain filter is a no-op (breakout conditions imply positive momentum).

eod_t's cheaper/broader universe includes small-caps that decouple from

### Other tests

- **NIFTYBEES SMA period sweep** (50/75/100/150/200): SMA(100) confirmed
  optimal by wide margin.
- **eod_t no min hold**: negligible impact (no regime exit to unlock).
- **eod_t realistic sizing** (4.5% avg turnover cap): -0.27pp CAGR,
  the fixed 1B cap was never binding.
- **eod_t ATSL no min hold**: slightly worse than ATSL mh=3.
