## What worked (improvements found)

### 1. Adaptive TSL for eod_technical (commit `6359d88`)

Config: `strategies/eod_technical/config_adaptive_tsl_30_3.yaml`

Once MFE from entry > 30%, trailing stop tightens from 10% to 3%.

| Metric | Champion | Adaptive | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| CAGR | 19.63% | 20.98% | +1.35pp |
| MDD | -25.95% | -24.68% | +1.27pp |
| Calmar | 0.757 | 0.850 | +0.093 |
| Sharpe | 1.067 | 0.995 | -0.072 |

Trade-off: loses ~25pp in sustained bull years (2021, 2023), gains
~20-39pp in volatile/choppy years (2019, 2020). Better MDD in 13/17
years. Not a dominant improvement — a risk-preference choice.

### 2. Drop direction_score from eod_breakout (commit `a1fb6ce`)

Config: `strategies/eod_breakout/config_no_ds_regime.yaml`

Direction_score disabled (threshold=0), regime gate retained.

| Metric | Champion | No DS | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| CAGR | 17.68% | 19.73% | +2.05pp |
| MDD | -26.75% | -27.52% | -0.77pp |
| Sharpe | 1.183 | 1.320 | +0.137 |
| Calmar | 0.661 | 0.717 | +0.056 |

DS was over-filtering: contrarian breakouts (stocks breaking out when
market breadth is low) are the strongest entries. Regime gate alone
provides sufficient macro protection.

## Per-trade feature analysis (Phase 5a/5b)

`scripts/build_trade_features.py` produces
`trade_features.parquet` (207k rows × 58 columns) per strategy.

`scripts/analyze_feature_pnl.py` produces
`docs/inspection/FEATURE_PNL_<strategy>.md` with quintile breakdowns.

Key finding: **the money leaks on the exit side, not the entry side.**
- Mean MFE: 13.85% (eod_b), 19.46% (eod_t)
- Mean give_back: 11.95% (eod_b), 16.03% (eod_t)
- Mean final pnl: 1.90% (eod_b), 3.44% (eod_t)

No pre-entry feature discriminates winners from losers with >3%
IS spread + IS↔OOS consistency. The entry clauses are already
well-saturated.

## Key architectural insights

1. **Position cap is the binding constraint** for both champions.
   99%+ of entry-passing candidates get blocked. Scanner/filter
   changes barely affect simulator-level output.
2. **Entry-only gating (direction_score) beats regime+force-exit**
   for eod_t's small-cap universe. Force-exit creates whipsaw.
3. **Regime+force-exit is essential for eod_b.** The regime gate
   provides macro protection AND generates alpha by cutting losers
   in bear regimes. But direction_score is redundant on top of it.
4. **Adaptive TSL fills the gap for strategies without regime exit.**
   eod_t lacks force-exit → adaptive TSL locks in gains. eod_b
   already has force-exit → adaptive TSL interferes.
