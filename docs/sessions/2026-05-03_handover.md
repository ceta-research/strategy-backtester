**Mechanism**: With ~15 positions continuously held, mark-to-market losses

force-exits, but by then 5-10% of MTM loss has accumulated. The DD is
**structural** to a continuously-exposed long-only strategy.

### 2. Three failed DD-reduction hypotheses

Ran each as post-hoc trade-log simulations:

#### Cooldown after regime flip
> Hypothesis: re-entries during chop are losers; skip entries for N days after each flip.

**Reality**: post-flip re-entries are AS profitable as other entries (avg PnL +3-4%). Skipping them HURTS CAGR. Dead end.

#### DD-based entry skip
> Hypothesis: skip entries when strategy is in deep DD (avoid catching falling knives).

**Reality**: trades entered when strategy DD < −12% have **WR 56% and avg PnL +7.98%** — they're the BEST trades. The strategy "buys the dip" effectively. Dead end.

#### Listing-age filter
> Hypothesis: skip newly-IPO'd stocks (using "first seen in trade history" as proxy for listing date).

**Reality**: at MIN_AGE=365d:
- Skipped 1530 of 2100 trades
- Saved 2026 (would skip all 38 trades, save Rs 9.8M)
- BUT cost 2021 (Rs 41.9M skipped winners), 2025 (Rs 47.6M), 2023 (Rs 39M)
- **Net: CAGR drops from 22.93% → 16.86%, Calmar 1.350 → 1.020**. Filter throws the baby out with the bathwater.

### 3. Combined filter (post-hoc): "skip new stocks IF in choppy regime"

Tested 24 (age, flip-threshold, lookback) combos via post-hoc simulation. Best:
- **(any age, ≥3 flips/30d)**: skipped only 2 trades over 16 years, Calmar 1.714 (vs 1.350)
- **(any age, ≥2 flips/30d)**: skipped 233 trades, Calmar 1.612

These looked promising. Implemented as engine parameter to validate.

### 4. Quiet-flip filter ENGINE IMPLEMENTATION (commit `141a41a`)

Added to `engine/signals/eod_breakout.py`:
- `entry_flip_skip_threshold` (default 999 = disabled)
- `entry_flip_lookback_days` (default 30)

Behavior: skip entry on date E when `flip_count_in_(E-N, E)` >= threshold.

**Byte-identical baseline** confirmed (filter disabled = legacy behavior).

**Real-engine sweep** results vs post-hoc simulation:

| Threshold / Lookback | Real engine | Post-hoc sim | Delta |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| ≥3 / 30d | Calmar 1.221 | 1.714 | −0.49 |
| ≥2 / 30d | Calmar 1.126 | 1.612 | −0.49 |
| ≥4 / 30d | Calmar 1.057 | (not tested) | — |
| ≥5 / 30d | Calmar 1.350 (= baseline) | — | — |
| ≥5 / 60d | Calmar 1.370 | — | +0.02 (marginal) |

**Critical finding: post-hoc simulation overestimated benefit by ~0.5 Calmar units.** Why?

In post-hoc, when a trade is skipped, its position slot stays empty. In the real engine, the slot is filled by the **next-ranked candidate** (by 180-day return). That next candidate is often a worse trade than the rejected one, so the "saved" loss is replaced by a different (often larger) loss.

**Conclusion: filter HYPOTHESIS does not survive empirical validation.** Engine code retained gated for future research; champion config unchanged.

### 5. 2026 YTD investigation

3-month YTD performance: −3.70% / WR 21% / PF 0.20. Not a strategy bug — three identifiable factors:

**Factor A: Regime whipsaw in progress**
- Three regime-flip cycles in 3 months (Jan 9, ~Feb 25, Mar 4)
- Flip count at each entry date: 0-1 flips in last 30d, 1 in last 60d
- The chop is just BEGINNING — no lookback filter can catch it before damage starts
- Pattern matches 2018 Q1 (which ended at −5.99% for the year)

**Factor B: Fresh-IPO universe**
- All 38 trades in 2026 are in stocks first seen 2025-2026
- 24 of 38 had `first_seen == entry_date` (true brand-new IPOs)
- Late-2025 IPO boom names: WEWORK, SMARTWORKS, ADVENTHTL, JAINREC, EMMVEE, AEGISVOPAK, EUROPRATIK, etc.
- These meet the liquidity scanner (Rs 7Cr/day) due to IPO-period volume but lack the price stability of established names

**Factor C: Listing-age filter would help 2026 specifically (-Rs 9.8M saved) but hurt total CAGR (-6pp).** Trade-off not worth it.

### 6. Convex ensemble weighting (commit `95d0e99`)

Added two new modes to `lib/ensemble_curve.py`:
- `convex_max_sharpe`: maximize μ'w / sqrt(w'Σw) at each rebalance
- `convex_target_vol`: maximize μ'w subject to w'Σw <= target_vol²

Both solve a convex QP via scipy SLSQP at each rebalance using trailing `weight_lookback_days` of leg returns. Anti-concentration cap (default 50% per leg).

**5-leg portfolio (eod_b + eod_t + low_pe + earnings_dip + qdt) results:**

| Variant | Window | CAGR | MDD | Sharpe | Calmar |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Baseline (2-leg invvol) | 2010-2026 | 18.79% | −23.81% | 1.281 | 0.789 |
| Convex Max-Sharpe (5yr lookback) | 2013-2026 | 16.35% | −15.78% | 1.131 | 1.036 |
| Convex Target-Vol 12% | 2013-2026 | 19.56% | −24.70% | 1.124 | 0.792 |
| Convex Max-Sharpe (2yr lookback) | 2013-2026 | 15.53% | −20.77% | 1.060 | 0.748 |

**MDD reduction is real (−24% → −16%) but at 2-3pp CAGR cost.** Calmar improves +31%.

**HOWEVER**: EOD-B IR-hyst SOLO (22.26% CAGR / −16.49% MDD / Calmar 1.350) **beats every ensemble combination tested.** Adding more strategies dilutes the IR-hyst edge.

**The 2-leg ensemble (production) uses the OLD eod_b champion** (`results/eod_breakout/champion.json` = 17.68% pre-IR-hyst version). If we updated it to use IR-hyst, the ensemble would likely improve significantly. Not done in this session — flagged for next session.
